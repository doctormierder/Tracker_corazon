# Project Memory

## Attempt 1: Binary Mask with Convex Hull [FAILED]
- **Date**: Early development
- **Method**: Topographic thresholding (16 strips along heart axis) -> binary mask.
  Thickness measured as distance between first and last white pixel along each
  perpendicular slice. Elastic memory (exponential smoothing) for stability.
- **Why it failed**: Binary mask discards all edge strength information. During
  systole, heart walls stretch and brighten (approach cavity intensity), causing
  the mask to incorrectly expand. Result: measured thickness INCREASES during
  contraction (opposite of physiology). Cells between heart and microscope
  create false edges that binary thresholding cannot distinguish from real walls.
- **Key lesson**: Intensity-only binary thresholding is fundamentally insufficient
  because wall brightness varies with stretch state.

## Attempt 2: Pure Intensity Gradient [FAILED]
- **Date**: 2026-05-12
- **Method**: Replaced binary mask with gradient-based edge detection on
  img_tratada (8-bit grayscale, processed through KitBiologico: CLAHE +
  bilateral + tophat + sobel + median + close). Found max positive gradient
  (dark->bright) and max negative gradient (bright->dark) per slice.
- **Why it failed**: img_tratada lacks sharp enough gradients for reliable edge
  detection. The heavy processing pipeline smooths edges, and the hex mask
  multiplication creates artificial gradients at its boundary. Result: no edges
  detected, static debug line, flat cardiogram signal.
- **Key lesson**: Processed images are not suitable for direct gradient edge
  detection; edges are too diffuse.

## Attempt 3: Hybrid (Binary Mask + Gradient Sub-pixel) [FAILED]
- **Date**: 2026-05-12
- **Method**: Binary mask for approximate edge positions, then sub-pixel
  gradient refinement (within +/-5px of each mask edge) using img_tratada
  intensity profile. Kept Gaussian slice weighting and Savitzky-Golay smoothing.
- **Why it failed**: The binary mask is the upstream weak link. It already
  gives wrong positions during systole (mask expands with bright walls). Sub-pixel
  refinement cannot fix fundamentally wrong initial estimates.
- **Key lesson**: The measurement method must be independent of binarization.
  Fixing the measurement alone is useless if the mask is corrupted by systole
  brightening.

## Current: Multi-cue Temporal Fusion [ACTIVE]
- **Date**: 2026-05-12
- **Method**: Combine 3 orthogonal evidence sources per pixel, per slice:
  1. Temporal stddev (60-frame rolling buffer of intensity profiles per slice)
     - High where walls oscillate (movement is independent of brightness)
     - Medium where cells move (they move slower than walls)
     - Low in static cavity and background
  2. Motion map (frame-to-frame absolute difference, blurred)
     - Instantaneous motion evidence (walls moving right now)
     - No lag, but noisier than temporal
  3. 4-bit confidence mask (16-level topographic threshold)
     - Inverted: (15 - level) = "not-cavity" probability
     - Smooth intensity gradients, preserves edge falloff
  Plus:
  - Adaptive weights (trust temporal more as buffer fills)
  - Per-slice quality score (reject slices with weak wall peaks)
  - Quality-weighted + Gaussian weighted mean across slices
  - Savitzky-Golay final smoothing (7-window, 3rd order)
- **Rationale**:
  - Temporal stddev solves the systole problem: walls always move, even when
    they brighten. Their movement creates variance that is independent of
    absolute intensity.
  - Cells between heart and microscope move slower than walls -> lower
    variance amplitude -> distinguishable from real walls.
  - Motion map bridges the first few frames before temporal buffer fills.
  - 4-bit mask preserves partial information about edge transitions,
    reducing jitter compared to binary threshold.
  - Quality score per slice handles axis misalignment during diastole: if
    the axis falls outside the thin cavity, only noise is seen on one side,
    and the slice is rejected.
- **Key insight from user**: "Noise" is actually cells between heart and
  microscope. They have consistent structure and move with the heartbeat but
  slower than the walls. This means variance AMPLITUDE (not just presence)
  separates them from walls.
- **Key insight from user**: The symmetry axis can be off-center during diastole
  (thin cavity). This produces asymmetric slices where one side has walls and
  the other has only cells. Solution: per-slice quality rejection.
- **Status**: Active development.
